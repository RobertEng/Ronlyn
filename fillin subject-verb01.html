Ronlyn's Subject/Verb Fill-in Responses

Verb: Go

Go (present simple tense)

These conjugations express actions that do not change.
  • I go out.
  • He goes out.
  • She goes out.
  • You go out.
  • We go out.
  • They go out.

Go (present continuous tense)

These conjugations express actions that are currently happening but will soon finish.
  • I am going out.
  • He is going out.
  • She is going out.
  • You are going out.
  • We are going out.
  • They are going out.

Go (past simple tense)

These conjugations express actions that just happened.
  • I went out.
  • He went out.
  • She went out.
  • You went out.
  • We went out.
  • They went out.

Go (past continuous tense)

These conjugations express actions that happened for some time.
  • I was going out.
  • He was going out.
  • She was going out.
  • You were going out.
  • We were going out.
  • They were going out.

Go (future simple tense)

These conjugations express imminent actions.
  • I will go out.
  • He will go out.
  • She will go out.
  • You will go out.
  • We will go out.
  • They will go out.

Go (future continuous tense)

These conjugations express actions that will be happening indeterminately.
  • I will be going out.
  • He will be going out.
  • She will be going out.
  • You will be going out.
  • We will be going out.
  • They will be going out.
